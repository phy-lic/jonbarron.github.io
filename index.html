Projects

I am broadly interested in many-body physics, nonequilibrium quantum dynamics, and statistical mechanics. Here are some selected projects.

Quantum chaos in a kicked Bose-Hubbard dimer model

I studied the integrability to chaos crossover in a kicked BH dimer model (which has a semicalassical counterpart) using spectral statistics and multifractality dimension analysis and studied rich local phase space dynamics using quantum information tools like OTOCs, entanglement entropy.

presentation
Effect of Disorder and Interactions in two site BH model

I studied the (counter-intuitive) phenomena of interaction-induced coherence in a disordered two-site Bose-Hubbard model. This minimal model captures enhancement of the superfluid-like regimes seen in full Bose-Hubbard model.

presentation
Variance Reduction in Large Time Correlations in Scalar Field Theory

We studied the signal-to-noise problem in large-time correlation functions of lattice (scalar) field theory. Using the analytically known distribution of bilocal correlators, we implemented a variance-reduction method that improves the extraction of effective masses from Monte Carlo simulations.

report
Theoretical Modelling of Gas Properties in Dark Matter Halos

I studied the Komatsu–Seljak model of intracluster gas in dark matter halos. Derived universal gas density and temperature profiles from hydrostatic equilibrium assumption and a polytropic equation of state. Additionally reproduced the Press–Schechter model of hierarchical structure formation and the resulting dark matter halo mass function. Investigated its dependence on cosmological parameters and cosmic time.

report  /  notes
Entanglement as a Resource

In this project, we review the conceptual foundations of quantum entanglement and its role as a nonclassical resource in communication and cryptography. We then demonstrate its practical applications through Superdense Coding, Quantum Teleportation and Quantum Key Distribution (BB84 and E91) using Qiskit and IBM Quantum hardware.
